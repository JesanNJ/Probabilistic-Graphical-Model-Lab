## Project Overview

This repository contains a literature review conducted as part of the course *Probabilistic Graphical Models for Data Science*. The objective is to explore current research trends in Probabilistic Graphical Models (PGMs) and analyze recent research contributions in the field.

Probabilistic Graphical Models provide a framework for representing complex probability distributions through graph structures. They are widely used in machine learning, artificial intelligence, computer vision, healthcare analytics, natural language processing, recommendation systems, and decision-making under uncertainty.

---

## Objectives

* Study current research trends in Probabilistic Graphical Models.
* Analyze recent research articles from leading journals and conferences.
* Understand methodologies, datasets, applications, and findings.
* Identify limitations and future research opportunities.
* Develop a structured literature survey for academic and research purposes.

---

## Research Questions

For each selected paper, the following questions are addressed:

1. What problem is addressed in the paper?
2. What methodology or model is used?
3. What dataset or application domain is considered?
4. What are the major findings or conclusions?
5. What limitations are mentioned?
6. What future research directions are suggested?

# Probabilistic Graphical Models: Research Trends and Applications

## Overview

This project explores the theoretical foundations, recent advancements, and practical applications of Probabilistic Graphical Models (PGMs) in modern Data Science and Artificial Intelligence. The study focuses on understanding how uncertainty, dependencies, and complex probabilistic relationships can be represented and analyzed through graphical structures.

Probabilistic Graphical Models combine graph theory and probability theory to provide a powerful framework for reasoning under uncertainty. They play a significant role in machine learning, decision-making systems, healthcare analytics, computer vision, natural language processing, recommender systems, and scientific modeling.

## Objectives

* Explore contemporary research trends in Probabilistic Graphical Models.
* Study advanced inference and learning techniques used in PGMs.
* Analyze the role of Bayesian and Markov-based approaches in modern AI systems.
* Investigate applications of probabilistic reasoning across multiple domains.
* Identify current challenges, limitations, and emerging research directions.

## Areas of Study

* Bayesian Networks
* Markov Random Fields
* Factor Graphs
* Probabilistic Inference
* Variational Inference
* Belief Propagation
* Deep Probabilistic Models
* Uncertainty Quantification
* Causal Reasoning
* Explainable Artificial Intelligence

## Key Outcomes

Through this project, a comprehensive understanding of probabilistic reasoning frameworks is developed, including their strengths, limitations, and real-world applicability. The study highlights how PGMs continue to evolve alongside deep learning and modern AI systems, enabling more interpretable, reliable, and uncertainty-aware machine learning solutions.
